2.9.16, a spoken-word poem

I could spend all day talking about what I like
Girls, chocolate, balling to be like Mike
But let's be real - here's what really matters
I don't want this to be all empty chatters
I have been blessed with family and friends
Their souls illuminate and their love transcends

Still there are days when I'm lost
Stuck in a world full of frost
I see the sun, I really do
It seems too good to be true
They say Icarus flew too close
To what he wanted the most
Is it really such a crime
To fight for what I feel is mine?
All I want is to leave my mark
Shake the seas like a whale shark

But I’m scared man, I'm scared
Of being an animal ensnared
Sometimes I feel like I don't belong
And I know I may not be Neil Armstrong
But I sure as hell could walk on the moon
Before you could say it's just a cartoon
That it's just another silly dream
No, you see, greatness is in my bloodstream
Yes, I may not always be understood
But I believe I'll end up in my version of Hollywood
Nothing great ever happened to those
Who tried to be like one of them everyday Joes'

There's a myth that tells a story
We once had four legs and four arms, looking for glory
Yet we were too powerful and so Zeus decided
It was time for us to be divided
He cursed us to forever roam the Earth
Until we found the half that gave us mirth
It's beautiful, don't you think?
To spend your whole life looking for that lost link
Yet it's scary to think about searching for her
When my whole future is such a blur
Truth be told I don't even know who I am
For all I know I could be one huge traffic jam

I'm scared man, I'm scared
Of being the one who dared
To do something extraordinary
Only to fail to complete the Hail Mary
In football if you lose you try again next year
But you only get one chance to live in this atmosphere
When night falls I gaze at the stars
And wonder if the whole world could be ours
Then I look at the people I'm surrounded by
And realize it's all sunshine and blue sky
